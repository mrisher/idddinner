Stir-Fried Beef and Sugar Snap Peas 
Makes: 4 Servings | 30 min | New York Times 
1 lb lean beef, cut into ¼-inch strips 
3 tbsp tamari 
2 tsp toasted sesame oil 
¼ tsp kosher salt 
½ tsp freshly ground black pepper 
1 lb sugar snap peas, trimmed 
3 fat scallions 
⅔ cup chicken broth 
2½ tbsp Madeira or sweet sherry 
1 tbsp cornstarch 
3 tbsp peanut or olive oil 
4 garlic cloves, minced 
Rice, for serving 
2 tbsp toasted sesame seeds 
Sriracha or other hot sauce, or rice 
wine vinegar for garnish 
chili oil, for garnish 
In a medium bowl, mix beef, 2 tbsps tamari, sesame oil, salt and pepper. Set 
aside. 
 
Thinly slice sugar snap peas crosswise into disks. Thinly slice scallions, reserving 
dark green parts for garnish. 
 
In a small bowl, mix chicken broth, Madeira, 2 tbsps water, remaining 1 tbsp 
tamari and cornstarch. 
 
Heat a large skillet over high heat. Add 2 tbsps oil. When pan is hot, stir-fry beef 
until browned, about 2 minutes. Transfer beef and any liquid to a plate. 
 
Add remaining tbsp oil to skillet and when hot, add garlic and white and light 
green scallion parts until lightly browned, about 1 minute. Add sugar snap peas 
and chicken broth mixture, lower heat to medium and cover. Let cook for 2 
minutes. Transfer beef and juices to skillet and stir-fry 2 minutes. Serve over rice, 
garnished with more sesame oil, sesame seeds, dark parts of scallions, and hot 
sauce or vinegar and chili oil.
